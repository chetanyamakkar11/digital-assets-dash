⬆ Methodology Validation: Schwab Launch Confirmed April 16, 2026
Our NLP model scored Charles Schwab at 19.81 in Q2 2025 — highest in our dataset — based on executives describing spot crypto trading plans and native custody infrastructure in earnings Q&A. On April 16, 2026, Schwab launched "Schwab Crypto" — direct BTC and ETH trading at 75bps. The model flagged this intent 10 months before the product launched. This confirms that Q&A NLP scores are predictive leading indicators, not trailing descriptions.
What We Analyzed

We scored Q&A sections of earnings call transcripts — where analysts press executives directly and hedged or absent answers are as informative as specific ones. 128 transcripts, 16 banks, Q1 2024–Q4 2025.

50 → 16 → Revised

Starting from 50 banks, NLP scoring produced 16 with measurable engagement. Post-analysis, 4 were reclassified as false positives (idiomatic "circle back" language). The composite model now surfaces 12 banks with genuine digital asset engagement.

Three Evidence Layers

Every cluster and composite score is supported by three independent sources: (1) original NLP pipeline, (2) Gemini-assisted research provided by our team, and (3) live web research conducted April 2026. No finding rests on a single source.

Scoring Framework
Methodology
4 dimensions
0–100 composite
We built a four-dimensional composite scoring system to address the fundamental weakness of single-metric NLP: it cannot distinguish a bank that says "crypto" 200 times from one that uses 12 specific product-level terms, and it misses operationally-live banks that underdisclose in Q&A. Each dimension is scored 0–25; composite is 0–100.
D1 — NLP Engagement Score (0–25)

Adjusted peak score from our NLP pipeline, log-normalised to 0–25. False positive terms (circle, strategic investment) removed. Log scale prevents Schwab's 19.81 from dominating — it maps to 24.9, while BNY's 9.70 maps to 19.5, preserving meaningful spread.

D1 = min(25, log(adj_peak+1) / log(21) × 25)
D2 — Specificity & Depth (0–25)

Addresses volume vs. depth: a bank saying "crypto" 200 times scores the same on D1 as one using 12 specific terms. D2 rewards breadth and product-specific vocabulary.

D2 = min(10, terms×0.85) + specificity×10 + consistency×5

Specificity = (Custodial+Infra scores) / total score
State Street: specificity=1.0 (all Custodial) · PNC: 0.048 (90% General)
D3 — Disclosure Mode (0–25)

The most novel dimension. Banks with QnA/Full-transcript ratio >2.0 are proactive disclosers — they discuss DA more in Q&A than prepared remarks, signalling investor-facing confidence. Goldman Sachs (ratio 0.23x) is an underdiscloser — its actual DA activity (GS DAP, tokenized MMFs) appears in scripted remarks but almost never gets raised in Q&A. This dimension surfaces that pattern systematically.

Citi ratio: 3.40x → D3=25 | BNY: 3.16x → D3=25
Goldman: 0.23x → D3=5.8 (corrected by D4)
D4 — External Research Score (0–25)

Explicitly labeled research-informed and analyst-judged. Scored on three sub-components: confirmed production products launched (0–10), infrastructure depth (0–8), and strategic trajectory (0–7). This dimension is what allows Goldman Sachs (D1=8.6) to reach composite 39.9 despite low NLP scores — its D4 of 22 reflects GS DAP spin-out, tokenized MMF with BNY, EIB digital bond, and $135M Digital Asset investment.

D4 = products_launched(0–10) + infrastructure_depth(0–8) + trajectory(0–7)

BNY: 10+8+7=25 | Goldman: 8+7+7=22 | TD Bank: 0+0+1=1
Why This Is Better Than Single-Score NLP
Goldman Sachs problem solved: Raw NLP ranks Goldman 11th (peak 1.84). Composite ranks it 11th but Tier 3 Building (39.9/100) with correct cluster assignment — its D4 of 22 compensates for systematic underdisclosure in Q&A. A pure NLP model would classify Goldman as "minimally engaged" when it is actually one of the most operationally active banks in capital markets tokenization.

PNC specificity problem solved: PNC's NLP peak is 5.87 (Developing), but its D2 specificity is 0.048 — meaning 95% of its score comes from General vocabulary ("crypto," "stablecoin" broadly). This correctly identifies PNC as a reactive discloser responding to analyst pressure, not an operational builder. Its composite (49.6) places it lower than US Bancorp (58.6) despite similar NLP peaks, because USB has a specificity ratio of 0.369 and active Custodial scores confirming real custody operations.

False positives isolated: Wells Fargo, Truist, BMO, TD Bank all have D1=0 (adjusted), D2=0, D3=0 after false positive removal. They reach composite scores of 1–9 only via D4 trajectory signals (WFUSD trademark, Bitcoin ETF access). This is the correct interpretation — these banks have planning signals but no operational engagement in our data window.

The GENIUS Act — Root Cause of Q2 2025 Inflection
The GENIUS Act was signed into law July 18, 2025 (Senate 68–30, House 308–122). It established the first federal regulatory framework for payment stablecoins: 1:1 reserves, mandatory audits, clear bank-subsidiary issuance pathway. 9 of 16 banks recorded their peak NLP score in Q2 2025 — this is the GENIUS Act's Senate passage (June 17) generating a wave of analyst stablecoin questions in Q2 earnings calls. The inflection is regulatory in origin, not coincidental.
Confirmed Developments Since Q4 2025 (Our Data Cutoff)
Cross-cluster development: PNC, Citi, and Wells Fargo are jointly exploring a stablecoin through Early Warning Services (Zelle's parent). This is three banks from three different clusters collaborating on shared infrastructure — a development our individual bank scores could not predict but our cluster analysis anticipated as a logical convergence between Clusters B and D.
